### AQA A Level Chemistry 1.1.2: Chemical and Physical Changes

A chemical change is where the chemical composition of the reactants changes to give products with different chemical, physical and often structural properties.

A physical change is where there is no change in the chemical composition of the reactants.

Examples of Physical change:

- The melting of ice and the formation of steam.
- Mixing of two liquids and the formation of a solution.
- The crushing of a solid to make a powder.

Examples of Chemical change:

- The burning of wood, producing carbon dioxide, water and ash.
- The oxidation of iron to form rust.
- The formation of a salt when a metal reacts with an acid.
- The formation of a salt when a metal reacts with a base.
